Local Heroes

Team: Dream Visions
Role: Logo Designer, Client Liaison

 For a popular sports bar in the area, my idea behind the logo was to capture the essence of Local Heroes through the lens of a sports fan in Ottawa.

Symbols of a hockey stick hitting a puck into a post form the abstraction of a cheerfully full beer-mug with foam spilling over the top. The elements also double as the first letters of Local Heroes to reinforce the brand by tying it into the theme and narrative. The colour palette of beer gold and ketchup red also subliminally remind the users of what they could be ordering when they visit this sports bar.

Logo for my Personal Brand


‘Out-of-the-box’ is how I wanted to reimagine myself as a brand.

I used a crisp but creative 3d perspective of my initials, ‘H’ and ‘G,’ to form a subtractive composition.

The G is open to light and ideas, which it lets through and projects on the ground as a circular arrow to symbolize my cycle of growth.


Scaredy Cat

A silly retake on the origin of the phrase, told through graphics for desktop, tablet and phone icons. This clumsy cat just wanted to play, but a bucket of orange paint and some yarn led to some scared friends.

Movie Poster

This poster composition, designed for an original thriller narrative shows a music game turned twisted survival-show with high stakes.

All shots were taken in one room, with one guitar and multiple perspectives on one camera and many photoshop effects. The elements are composed to strategically lead the eye through a sequence of all elements across the poster to convey the narrative.

Kayotea

My entry for a competition to rebrand an existing Algonquin-based tea company tried to capture the essence of recurring motifs and forms in Algonquin art and reinterpret them in the form of a typeface logo.
