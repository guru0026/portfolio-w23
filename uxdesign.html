User Experience Design

Existing websites and apps reimagined through wireframes designed on Adobe XD

NASA Photo Library (ongoing)

Adobe XD Link
For this ongoing project, we have to design a website which allows users to browse NASA’s photo database by date.

The gradient from stratosphere-blue to space-black forms the backdrop for golden elements inspired by the lunar module’s foil, and gray from the moon’s craters and NASA’s space shuttles. The thin, crisp and minimal lines capture the weightlessness and futuristic outlook of NASA. The font used the for the headings draws inspiration from NASA’s ‘worm’ logo typeface and also gives a sense of futuristic minimalism.

Elements on the website are designed to give a sense of excitement about travel, whether it’s through time in NASA’s libraries or through space.

Crackle

Adobe XD Link
For this theoretical project, we were asked to rebrand and redesign an existing mobile app.

My reinterpretation of the streaming platform crackle captures the experience of a campfire story.

Symbolism like the crackling of the campfire, and the roasting marshmallow inside the logo’s “C” makes users feel like they’re getting ready for an immersive tale when they browse the apps for movies and shows.

H&R Pizza

Adobe XD Link
In this rebranding and redesigning assignment of an existing website, I tried to capture the experience of Italian fast-food with the sauce-red, herbal-green and creamy yellows of the colour palette.

The organic, spaghetti-like curves of the logo’s typeface, and the subtle patterns in the background are meant to provide context and build the user’s appetite.

The organized sections and visual menu makes it easy for users to find and decide on items.
